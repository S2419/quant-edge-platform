How the model works

We use a multi-layer ensemble model. Layers include Dixon-Coles match probability fitting, Elo ratings, expected-goals divergence (rolling xG vs actual finishing), weather effects on goal totals, confirmed lineup/absence gating, referee discipline profiles, head-to-head venue patterns, and others.

We don't reveal the exact layer count, weights, thresholds, or layer names — that's the only piece of IP we have, and giving it away would kill the edge. What we do reveal: every bet, every stake, every edge %, every win and loss, in real time.

The bets are public. The results are settled in real time. The method stays private.

Methodology limitations (be honest about it)

103 bets is suggestive, not statistically significant at the p<0.05 level. With this sample size, the true edge could plausibly sit anywhere from +5% (real but smaller than headline) to +25% (lucky sample). We'd need 500+ settled bets to make a definitive statistical claim. We're publishing the track record as it builds — you're watching the experiment run, not buying a finished proof.

Other caveats worth knowing: the bankroll is small (~£11.30 current), so stake variance is high in percentage terms. Closing-line value is backfilled for 83 of the 103 bets so far. The Cards market is still in micro-validation (10p flat) and shouldn't be treated as a proven layer yet. If those caveats kill your interest, that's the correct response — better now than after £25.
